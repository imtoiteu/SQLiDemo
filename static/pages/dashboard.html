✅ What bcrypt protects

Even if an attacker gains full database access, they cannot see or reverse the original passwords. Each hash is unique due to random salting.

❌ What was exposed

Usernames, emails, and roles are still visible and exploitable — this data can be used for phishing, social engineering, and targeted attacks.

📖 Lesson Learned

Always hash passwords with a strong algorithm (bcrypt, argon2). Never store plaintext. Minimize data collection to reduce breach impact.
